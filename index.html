Executive Summary

Our 2025 Capital Market Assumptions reflect a world transitioning from a decade of extraordinary monetary accommodation toward a structurally higher nominal rate environment. After the most aggressive global tightening cycle since the early 1980s, central banks have begun cutting rates, but our 10-year horizon assumptions embed a "higher for longer" neutral rate framework — with the Fed's r* settling near 3.0% (down from 3.25% last year as recent data suggests modest downside to neutral). The combination of sticky services inflation, slowing but resilient growth, and elevated sovereign debt loads defines the macro regime. Equity returns are modestly reduced from 2024 CMAs, while fixed income offers meaningfully better carry than the prior decade. Private markets remain attractive on an absolute basis but face a valuation recalibration cycle as public comps have corrected.

Macroeconomic Framework

Theme 1: The Great Normalization

After 15 years of zero interest rate policy and quantitative easing, the global economy is navigating a structural normalization of both policy rates and inflation expectations. We expect US CPI to average 2.7% over the 10-year horizon (revised down from 3.0% in our 2024 CMAs), as goods deflation from supply chain normalization offsets persistent services and shelter inflation. The Fed's terminal rate assumption is 3.0%, implying real short rates of approximately 30-50 basis points.

Theme 2: Fiscal Dominance Risk

US federal debt/GDP exceeds 120% and the structural primary deficit is estimated at 3-4% of GDP. This creates upward pressure on term premiums and reduces the Fed's ability to ease aggressively in downturns. We embed a term premium of ~30bps in our 10yr Treasury assumption (ACM model: NY Fed), down from 35bps in 2024.

Theme 3: Multipolar Growth

China's secular deceleration (4.4% vs 4.8% prior year) as the property sector restructures and demographics turn definitively adverse represents the largest YoY change to our growth framework. India (6.5% real growth) and Southeast Asia partially offset this, but the EM aggregate growth assumption falls from 6.3% to 6.0% weighted.

Theme 4: AI and Productivity

A new element in this year's framework is an explicit adjustment for AI-driven productivity. We incorporate a modest 0.2% annual upside to US real EPS growth from AI-enabled corporate productivity, though we note this is highly uncertain.

Theme 5: Energy Transition

The capital expenditure supercycle in clean energy, grid infrastructure, and critical minerals supports infrastructure, commodities, and select real assets above their historical return profiles. We raise our commodity risk premium from 0.50% to 1.00% YoY.

Equity Outlook

US Large Cap

Arithmetic return revised to 8.2% from 8.6% in 2024 CMAs. The primary driver of the 40bp reduction is a higher CAPE mean reversion assumption. The dividend yield has compressed to 1.36%, as buyback-heavy S&P composition continues to shift from dividends to capital returns.

Intl Developed

Return assumption raised to 8.9% from 8.7%, the only major asset class with a YoY upgrade. European equities enter the decade at ~13x CAPE. Japan is now a separate positive contributor as real wage growth turns positive for the first time since the 1990s.

Emerging Markets

Return forecast at 10.2%, modestly lower than 10.3% in 2024. China repricing risk offsets the attractive starting valuations in India, Brazil, and ASEAN. Net, EM is still the highest-return public equity asset class on a geometric basis.

Fixed Income & Alternatives

Fixed Income Overview

The most important story in fixed income is the return of income. Our 10-year US Treasury yield assumption of 4.4% reflects modest normalization from current spot levels. The full fixed income complex earns 4.4-7.2% arithmetic depending on credit risk.

US IG Bonds

Expected return of 4.9% — unchanged from 2024 CMAs. For intermediate duration bonds, starting yield is the primary return driver over a 10-year horizon.

US High Yield

Return of 6.8%, down 20bps from 7.0%. HY spreads at 340bps are below the historical median of ~450bps. We embed modestly higher default loss assumptions (1.40% net loss vs 1.30%).

TIPS

Our most upgraded fixed income asset at +20bps vs 2024 (4.1% from 3.9%). The positive real yield of 1.75% represents a genuine store of inflation-adjusted purchasing power.

Alternatives & Private Markets

Private Equity

Return assumption of 12.0% (gross of taxes), down from 12.5%. The 50bp reduction reflects public equity PME base down 40bps and leverage premium modestly reduced. The illiquidity premium is unchanged at 250bps.

Private Credit

Upgraded to 9.5% from 9.8%. This remains our best Sharpe ratio asset class among private markets. The structural shift of lending from banks to private credit continues to support deal flow.

Real Estate

Revised down to 7.8% from 8.2%. Cap rate expansion creates a better income entry point, but NOI growth has slowed. Industrial and residential dominate our positive outlook; office remains structurally challenged.

Hedge Funds

Net return of 7.0%, down from 7.5%. Alpha assumption reduced from 1.5% to 1.0% net. Multi-strategy and relative value maintain their position as efficient risk-adjusted vehicles.

Commodities

Upgraded from 4.2% to 4.8%, driven by the increase in the risk premium from 50bps to 100bps. The energy transition capex cycle creates structurally tighter supply/demand balances.

Internal Consistency Checks

(1) EQUITY RISK PREMIUM CONSISTENCY: The ERP implied by our return assumptions minus the risk-free rate (US T-Bill at 3.9%) equals approximately 4.3% for US LC equity — consistent with Damodaran's January 2025 implied ERP of 4.2-4.5%.

(2) CREDIT SPREAD / DEFAULT CONSISTENCY: Our HY total return assumption of 6.8% is consistent with: YTM of ~7.2% minus expected default loss of 1.4% = 5.8% net plus roll of ~1.0% = 6.8%.

(3) PRIVATE vs PUBLIC CONSISTENCY: PE return (12.0%) = Public equity PME (8.2%) + Illiquidity (2.5%) + Leverage (2.0%) + Alpha (1.0%) – Fees (1.7%).

(4) REAL RATE / INFLATION CONSISTENCY: US TIPS real yield (1.75%) + Breakeven inflation (2.65%) = Nominal Treasury yield (4.40%).

(5) TERM PREMIUM CONSISTENCY: The implied term premium (10yr yield minus expected short rate path) is approximately 30bps, consistent with the NY Fed ACM model.

(6) CORRELATION MATRIX POSITIVE SEMI-DEFINITENESS: The correlation matrix has been verified to be positive semi-definite (all eigenvalues ≥ 0) using the Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage estimator.
